Sydney Silchester, that has moved to a small village in Sussex, seeks out local honey and ends up entangled in murder when his supplier is found dead, apparently stung by her own bees. He seeks help from his neighbour Mr. Mycroft, a retired gentleman on the Sussex Downs interested in beekeeping. The name is of course suggestive and at one point Mycroft admits that "Mycroft is only one of my family names."

This is an early Sherlock Holmes pastiche, first published in 1941 and it is told from the viewpoint of Silchester. The mystery centres on whether killer bees could have been deliberately trained as a murder weapon.
